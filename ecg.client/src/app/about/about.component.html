What is Tyre Retreading?

Tyre retreading is a process of renewing worn-out tyres by applying a new tread, giving them a second life. It is a sustainable and economical alternative to buying new tyres, as it reuses the original casing while providing new tread patterns for enhanced grip and durability. Retreaded tyres are widely used in commercial and fleet vehicles due to their cost-efficiency and reliability.

Types of Retreading

Conventional or Hot Retreading

Hot retreading, also known as conventional retreading, involves applying a new tread to a tyre using heat and pressure. This method is ideal for tyres that undergo heavy usage and need strong adhesion between the new tread and the casing. It is a tried-and-tested process that provides a durable and long-lasting solution for heavy-duty vehicles.

Cold or Precured Retreading

Cold retreading, also known as precured retreading, involves bonding a precured tread to the casing without the application of heat. This method is preferred for its consistent tread quality and is widely used in commercial vehicles. Cold retreading provides superior traction and is more energy-efficient, making it an eco-friendly option.
